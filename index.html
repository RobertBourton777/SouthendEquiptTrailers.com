Name Description
EQUIPTER 4000 Formerly known as the RB4000, the self-propelled and towable Equipter 4000 is powered by a gasoline engine that takes it virtually anywhere on a job site. With a tight turning radius to navigate difficult spots and evenly distributed weight, it reduces the risk of property damage—and it won’t leave ruts in customers’ lawns. The Equipter 4000’s 12-foot lift does the heavy lifting for you. Whether you’re lifting materials to the roof or lowering two tons of shingle debris to the ground, this roofing equipment improves your efficiency and maintains a much cleaner job site. Lower the risk of property damage with its 4-foot roll-back feature, which allows you to position the container up and over customers’ flower beds, bushes, and other exterior features. When the nearly 4,000-lb capacity container is full, position the Equipter 4000 lift next to a trailer, truck, or dumpster under 12 feet high. Then simply raise the container and unload it using the hydraulic tailgate.
EQUIPTER 3300 This mobile container’s front wheels lift and drive axles disengage, allowing you to easily transport it from job site to job site by hitching it to your truck, just like any other trailer. Use less shovel power and more Equipter power with a machine that will haul, spread, or position up to 4,740 pounds of materials, including dirt, rocks, mulch, plants, and trees. Its hinged tailgate and rear stabilizers combine for no-hassle dumping, even on uneven terrain. With a rust-proof aluminum body that measures about 6 feet wide by 13 feet long, the compact Equipter 3300 trailer hauls materials places you wouldn’t dare take bulky trailers, big dumpsters, or work trucks. Use its precision turning to maneuver around tight spots, like established landscaping or residential lots. Any similarities with other trailers end once the Equipter 3300 trailer is on the job site. Engage its gas-powered engine to drive and position this rugged contracting equipment on construction sites as well as residential and commercial properties.
EQUIPTER 2500 Transport the Equipter 2500 lift on a utility trailer, just like other contracting equipment. Once you’re on site, fire up its gas-powered engine to navigate this compact dumpster through alleyways, down narrow sidewalks and stone paths, nearly anywhere it's needed. After filling the 1.76 cubic yard container, use the lift’s engine to drive the aluminum container to an on-site dump truck, trailer, or another disposal spot. The easy-to-operate control panel lets you raise the lightweight container up to 7 feet to easily dump the debris via the hydraulic tailgate. On-site debris disposal really can be as simple as that. This compact, lightweight dumpster is specifically designed to fit in places other contractor equipment can’t. Its 4-foot 4-inch wide chassis and easy maneuverability allows you to navigate into hard-to-reach areas, like alleyways, to catch falling debris. Lift materials up to your level with the Equipter 2500’s 7-foot lift and secure the sturdy stabilizers to position the aluminum container right where you need it. This also cuts the distance debris will fall, helping reduce the risk of damage to your customer's property.
EQUIPTER 2000 Transport the Equipter 2000 lift on a trailer, just like other contracting equipment. Once you’re onsite, fire up its gas-powered engine to drive this super-compact dumpster precisely where you need it. When the 1.13 cubic yard container is full, use the lift’s engine to drive it to an onsite truck, trailer, or other disposal spot. Raise this equipment up to 7 feet to easily dump debris via the hinged tailgate. Disposal really is that simple. This small, moveable dumpster is specifically engineered to go places other contractor’s equipment can’t. With a chassis under 3 feet wide and easy maneuverability, navigate into hard-to-reach areas, whether you’re roofing the porch on a city side street or renovating the interior of a commercial building. Get the job done faster by taking advantage of the Equipter 2000’s 7-foot lift and sturdy stabilizers to position the aluminum container closer to the second floor or roof. Because you’re no longer tossing materials all the way to the ground, you reduce the risk of damaging the client’s home or property.
TOW-A-LIFT Tired of the hassle of transporting traditional forklifts? Transporting a lift has never been easier. Simply hitch the Equipter Tow-A-Lift to a vehicle just like a normal trailer. With a tongue weight of 750 pounds and a total empty weight of 2,500 pounds, the Tow-A-Lift does not require a trailer or specialized equipment to move from site to site. Lift heavy things with ease. The Equipter Tow-A-Lift can lift up to 3,500 pounds with a boom arm whose range of motion is similar to that of a telehandler. This towable forklift can fit a 42” wide pallet between its wheels or can carry a larger load above its base. The Equipter Tow-A-Lift is self-propelled and remote controlled, allowing the operator to view it from any angle. The 6’7” wide unit is small enough to move through tight access areas and its large tires and body design evenly distribute weight, allowing the Tow-A-Lift to operate without disturbing landscaping.
EQUIPTER 5400 Once on site, unhitch the compact crane and start its gas-powered engine. Drive and position the equipment virtually anywhere so you work smarter, not harder. This mobile crane eliminates the hassle of moving from job site to job site. With front wheels that lift and drive axles that disengage, you simply hitch the Equipter 5400 crane to your truck, just like any other trailer. Formerly the CR8400, the Equipter 5400 crane does the heavy lifting for you. Use its 23-foot extendable arm and up to 4,431-lb capacity to raise and lower roofing materials, drywall, cabinetry, pre-formed fixtures, windows, and more. Tight spots are no problem for this compact crane. With a 6-foot wide chassis, precision turning, wide tires, and independently operated outriggers, you get the job done while reducing the risk for property damage.
